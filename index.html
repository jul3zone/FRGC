What are the benefits?

Chickens lay eggs.

The eggs are fresh, taste great, and are full of nutrition because you control what goes into egg production with the food you give your chickens.

They create excellent fertilizer.

Chicken manure contains a good deal of nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium – the primary ingredients in lawn and garden fertilizers. They're even willing to do this for free!

Chickens make great pets.

chickens have their own individual personalities and are affectionate toward humans. Each is its own bird, as they say, with some being sweet, shy, grouchy, or playful.

Chickens help clean your yard.

Chickens can be put in a lawn tractor and moved around the yard to help dethatch and clean up underground pests. They can also be placed in weeded garden beds to eat weeds!

Chickens are like a natural garbage disposal.

Chickens can eat many of the food items we would normally throw away, including food scraps from salads, vegetable peelings, rice, fruits, nuts, and seeds.

Chickens are fairly easy to take care of.

A low-maintenance animal, chickens basically need to be fed, watered, and given a coop to nest in.

Chickens help control bugs and weeds naturally.

Without the use of chemicals and insecticides, you can control over-population of crickets, grasshoppers, snails, slugs and other pests in your garden as chickens eat them up.

They enable freedom

Grocery store eggs come from factory farms, with chickens living in cramped and inhumane conditions. Having backyard hens gives you the freedom to not support this type of farming and eat healthier at lower cost.
